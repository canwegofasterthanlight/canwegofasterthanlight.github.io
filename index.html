Can We Go The Speed Of Light?

Short answer, no.
Long answer, yes theoretically.

With our current understanding of physics and the speed of light, nothing with a mass can travel the speed of light (3x108ms-1). This was first theorised by Einstein with his thought experiment. Einstein's conclusion was that your length would become zero and you would have an infinite mass due to the warping in space-time. Having said infinite mass also means it needs an infinite amount of kinetic energy to accelerate to 3x108ms-1, therefore making it impossible for all particles of mass (The relativity of simultaneity).

This outcome is because of the theory of relativity and its effects from two observers . However, many physicists including Einstein agree that any physical theory is an approximation of reality and not a literal description. For example, if we go small, really small, ignoring the theory of relativity, we end up with quantum mechanics.

Some parts of quantum mechanics still marry up with the theory of relativity, for example: quantum field theory. Nevertheless, most quantum mechanics exists outside of it, including one key mechanic that disproves the so-called “speed limit of the universe” - Quantum Entanglement. In experiments, the effects of entanglement have been calculated going 10,000 times the speed of light. If we theorise quantum entanglement communication were possible, as well as the ability to turn matter into data and vise-versa, it would be possible to transfer you from one place to another at faster than the speed of light.
